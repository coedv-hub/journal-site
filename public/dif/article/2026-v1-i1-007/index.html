Abstract

As artificial intelligence and natural language processing (NLP) systems become increasingly integrated into our daily lives, addressing gender bias in these systems has become crucial for ensuring fair and equitable technology. Our team mainly participated in Subtask 1: Bias Detection, requiring the model to judge whether a sentence is gender-biased (B), or non-biased (N), and Subtask 2: Bias Classification, which involves classifying the gender-biased sentences into different categories, including gender stereotyped activity and career choices (AC), gender stereotyped descriptions and inductions (DI), and expressed gender stereotyped attitudes, norms and beliefs (ANB). We mainly used various fine-tuning pre-trained models or ensemble learning methods of pre-trained models and achieved remarkable results in this field, ranking 4th and 3rd respectively in the two subtasks. Corresponding to the task setting team and the official dataset results from NLPCC 2025 Shared Task 7-CORGI-PM 2025: Chinese Corpus for Gender Bias Detection, Classification and Mitigation Challenge. The project source code is available from https://github.com/wangkongqiang/nlpcc2025_task7.
