Abstract

Sichuan’s cultural tourism sector faces overlapping challenges, including peak-season visitor surges, mountainous transportation bottlenecks, and governance constraints in ecologically sensitive areas. Under these conditions, conventional checklist-style scenic-spot recommendations often fail to translate into executable itineraries. This paper proposes the “Bashu Cultural Narrative Chain” as an experiential backbone. Using cultural symbols as thematic threads, it connects interpretation, experience, service, and communication across the pre-arrival, on-site, and post-departure phases, while incorporating carrying capacity, time windows, and risk as system inputs. Building on this structure, the paper advances an intelligent matching approach oriented toward destination operations: structuring people, places, events, objects, arts, and flavors through knowledge graphs; balancing experience enhancement and governance constraints via multi-objective optimization; improving narration and multilingual service efficiency through AIGC; and embedding trust mechanisms—including fact verification, copyright compliance, and AI-generated content labeling—into the content generation and service workflow. Through a comparative analysis of three scenario types—hard-constraint management at Jiuzhaigou, content-driven urban night tours in Chengdu, and weekend micro-trips in the Chengdu–Chongqing metropolitan area—the paper demonstrates that intelligent matching must adjust the prioritization of content and constraints in response to context in order to be practically viable.
