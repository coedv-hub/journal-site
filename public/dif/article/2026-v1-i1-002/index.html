Research on the Construction of a Data-Driven Evaluation System for General Artificial Intelligence Courses in Higher Vocational Education

Abstract

With the rapid advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) technology, the education sector is undergoing profound transformations. The teaching quality of general AI courses in higher vocational colleges is pivotal to the improvement of students’ digital literacy and their long-term career development. Nevertheless, the current evaluation systems for such courses suffer from prominent drawbacks, including single evaluation modes, delayed feedback mechanisms, the absence of dynamic learning monitoring, and insufficient personalized evaluation design. Based on the theory of multiple intelligences, this study constructs a full-process evaluation system covering pre-class, in-class and post-class teaching stages. Preliminary evaluation indicators are obtained through expert questionnaires, and key indicators are extracted via dimensionality reduction using the principal component analysis (PCA) method. Furthermore, the grey wolf optimization (GWO) algorithm is adopted to optimize the hyperparameters of the random forest model for determining the weight of each evaluation indicator, which effectively improves the accuracy and efficiency of course evaluation. The research findings can provide a scientific basis and practical guidance for the quality improvement of general AI courses in higher vocational colleges, and support the personalized development and comprehensive ability cultivation of students.
