Abstract

The TPACK model provides an integrated perspective of technology, pedagogy and content knowledge for programming education in kindergartens. Integrating the TPACK perspective into Bee-Bot programming robot activities enables teachers to observe and respond to the development of children's sequential thinking, create a free exploration programming environment for children, and empower the growth of their logical sequencing abilities. Taking "Bee-Bot Treasure Hunt" as an example, teachers guide children through four stages - situational friction, independent planning, tool exploration, and experience creation. In each stage, children engage in multiple exploratory programming attempts, from which they distill one key insight about sequential thinking. This process continuously strengthens the sequential thinking ability of senior kindergarten children.
