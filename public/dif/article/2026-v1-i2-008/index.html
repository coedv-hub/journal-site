Abstract

When introducing AI-based recommendation systems into holistic personality education, a fundamental contradiction emerges: the click-through rates and dwell time maximized by commercial recommendation logic often run counter to the heart-mind cultivation sought by education. Content that best captivates students frequently avoids real challenges to core qualities such as concentration, resilience, and discernment; while tasks genuinely capable of stretching students' heart-mind weaknesses may bring frustration and cognitive discomfort, scoring poorly in traditional recommendation models. To resolve this problem, this paper proposes a comprehensive intelligent recommendation decision framework. It constructs a four-dimensional student profile and reconstructs the utility function of the recommendation system, giving value activation and heart-mind targeting greater importance than cognitive fit and interest match. The Heart-Mind Bandit algorithm replaces immediate reward signals with cultivation gain quantified by physiological and behavioral data. This work marks a transition of AI's role in education from catering to desires to safeguarding growth, providing an interpretable and auditable paradigm for responsible AI decision-making in values education.
