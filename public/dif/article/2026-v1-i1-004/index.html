Abstract

To address the core problems of inadequate personalization and insufficient professional alignment in college mathematics teaching, this study constructs a trinity integrated framework of "intelligent teaching system, personalized resource system, and blended teaching model" based on constructivist learning theory and precision teaching theory. A one-semester empirical study was conducted on four classes of the 2023 Mechanical Design, Manufacturing and Automation major at Chengdu Technological University, with 86 students in the experimental group and 82 in the control group. Rigorous statistical analyses were performed using the Shapiro-Wilk normality test, Levene homogeneity test, independent-samples t-test, Pearson chi-square test, and Bonferroni multiple correction. Results show that the final average score of the experimental group was significantly higher than that of the control group. Multiple linear regression analysis confirmed that the AI-integrated teaching model had a significant independent effect on final scores. Findings reveal that the core mechanism of AI-enabled teaching lies in data-driven precise learning diagnosis and professionally adapted personalized instruction, while the improvement of teachers' technological literacy and collaborative construction of teaching resources are critical guarantees for teaching reform.
