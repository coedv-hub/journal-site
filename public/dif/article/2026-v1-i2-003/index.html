Abstract

Against the backdrop of the comprehensive and in-depth advancement of the digital education strategy, mathematics teaching in rural junior high schools across Guangxi has long been constrained by practical bottlenecks, including monotonous teaching resources, difficulties in visualizing abstract knowledge, poorly tailored after-class assignments, and inefficient feedback mechanisms. These issues have further led to prominent problems such as the difficulty in implementing targeted teaching, the hindered development of students' abstract thinking, and low classroom engagement. Based on national and regional policies for applying artificial intelligence to education, this paper systematically analyzes the feasibility and necessity of the in-depth integration of artificial intelligence with mathematics teaching in rural junior high schools of Guangxi. Taking linear equations with one variable as a teaching case, it elaborates on applications of AI lesson preparation, dynamic visualization, and intelligent question-answering tools throughout the teaching process. Comparative analysis verifies the value of AI tools in resolving teaching difficulties, improving teaching efficiency, and boosting students' learning outcomes. The results provide a replicable practical model for the digital transformation of basic education in underdeveloped areas.
