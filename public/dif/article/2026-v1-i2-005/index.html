Abstract

With the rapid advancement of AI, big data, and IoT, industrial transformation has entered a new phase of digital intelligence, raising higher competency demands for talent. As the primary base for cultivating high-skilled workers, skill-oriented higher education institutions face both opportunities and challenges for systemic change. This paper analyzes the drivers, dilemmas, and pathways of digital intelligence-empowered transformation from three perspectives. The study identifies three intrinsic drivers: industrial upgrading as demand-side pull, national policies and contemporary development as environmental traction, and institutional system change as organizational adaptation. Realistic dilemmas include internal adaptive obstacles among institutions, teachers, and students, as well as external structural imbalances in university-enterprise and inter-institutional relationships. To address these, a four-dimensional collaborative pathway - concept, competence, relationship, and institution - is proposed. This study offers systematic theoretical and practical guidance for the digital intelligence transformation of skill-oriented higher education institutions.
