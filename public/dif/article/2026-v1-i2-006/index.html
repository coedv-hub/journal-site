Abstract

As global sustainable development advances, green energy plays an increasingly important role in modern commercial space design. This paper discusses the ecological value and spatial transformation brought by applying green energy to such design, and analyzes its effects on ecological benefits as well as the restructuring of spatial functions and forms. It first introduces the concept of green energy, explains its importance in modern architecture and its inherent link with commercial space planning. Combined with theoretical analysis and case studies, the paper further explores the ecological strengths of green energy in commercial spaces, such as higher energy efficiency, lower carbon emissions and better ecological service performance.
