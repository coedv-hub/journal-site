Abstract

Against the backdrop of the vigorous development of digital technology, this study depicts the foundational support for the high-quality development of cross-border e-commerce in Xinjiang by presenting operational data and institutional practices of cross-border e-commerce in 6 major node cities of Xinjiang from 2024 to 2025, as well as the current status of infrastructure and industrial foundation in the region. It further analyzes realistic challenges restricting the empowerment effect of digital technology from three dimensions: the depth of digital technology application, policy coordination and talent supply, and industrial ecological synergy. The research shows that cross-border e-commerce in Xinjiang has achieved explosive growth driven by digital technology and policy dividends, yet there exists a structural contradiction of "strong hardware yet weak software". On this basis, this study proposes a three-dimensional collaborative optimization strategy of "digital infrastructure - policy system - industrial integration" to address the current realistic constraints on the empowerment effect of digital technology, so as to provide decision-making references for Xinjiang to build a highland for inland and border opening-up.
