From Quantitative Indexes' Stability to that of Stylometry: A Homogeneous Texts Approach to EFL Academic Writing

Abstract

We begin to verify the hypothesis that EFL teachers who have published academic writing have a stable writing style for homogeneous written texts, as revealed by the stability of quantitative indexes. To achieve this, we analyzed ten homogeneous academic texts from an EFL academic monograph and 26 homogeneous academic texts from the academic papers of three EFL teachers, calculating quantitative indexes using the Quantitative Index Text Analyser (QUITA) software. Pearson correlation and principal component analysis were used to analyze the major quantitative indices of writing styles. The results show that all the principal quantitative indices, excepting verb distances, maintain a high degree of stability, reflecting the stable writing style of the author. Writer's view, activity, and thematic concentration, as well as entropy, descriptivity, and thematic concentration, can represent the individual's writing style. The verified hypothesis can be further applied to help identify EFL written text authorship and contribute to academic writing ethics education. In this way, we suggest that self-checking policy in higher education be adopted to promote education development before publishing academic writing.
